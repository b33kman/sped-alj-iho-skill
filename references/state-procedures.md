> **Important**: This reference is for procedural context only. Always verify current
> rules against official state regulations. Do not rely on this file as a definitive
> legal source — it is a working reference to orient session context.

## Federal Baseline (IDEA)

These timelines apply in all states unless a state has adopted stricter rules:

| Requirement | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Resolution session (after complaint filed) | Within 15 days |
| Resolution period | 30 days (waivable) |
| Due process hearing decision | 45 days after resolution period ends |
| State-level appeal decision | 30 days after appeal filed |
| Civil action filing window | 90 days from final decision (or state law) |

**Key IDEA provisions relevant to hearings:**
- 20 U.S.C. § 1415 — Procedural safeguards
- 34 C.F.R. §§ 300.507–300.516 — Due process procedures
- Stay-put (pendency) obligation during proceedings: 34 C.F.R. § 300.518

## New York

**Administering agency**: Impartial Hearing Officers (IHOs) appointed by school districts; state review by State Review Officer (SRO)
**Governing law**: Education Law § 4404; 8 NYCRR Part 200

| Requirement | Timeline |
|---|---|
| IHO decision | 45 days after final hearing date |
| SRO appeal | Filed within 35 days of IHO decision |
| SRO decision | 30 days after record is closed |
| Civil action | Within 4 months of SRO decision (CPLR) |

**Notes:**
- New York uses a two-tier system: IHO → SRO → federal/state court.
- IHOs are selected from a rotating list maintained by the district.
- Pendency (stay-put) is strictly enforced; interim relief motions are common.
- NYC has a dedicated impartial hearing unit (IHSRO).
